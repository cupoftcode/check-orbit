CheckOrbit · Design system

The system behind fast, trustworthy compliance answers.

CheckOrbit's design is a travel guidebook crossed with modern SaaS: warm cream, burnt-orange confidence, steel-blue authority, and a strictly separated four-lane status system for compliance verdicts. This is the canonical reference.

02 · Voice & copy

Clarity is the empathetic choice.

CheckOrbit handles high-stakes medical compliance. Travelers arrive at 2 AM, 72 hours before departure, looking for a real answer. Soft language is not kind — it is dangerous. Lead with the verdict. Name the consequence. Hand them the next step.

Say this

Banned. Do not carry.
Amphetamine is prohibited under Japan's Stimulants Control Act. No tourist permit exists.
Switch to a non-stimulant alternative before Apr 25.

Verdict first. Legal basis second. Actionable deadline third.

Never this

This medication may potentially have some restrictions…
Unfortunately, we regret to inform you that…
Please consult a professional for more information.

Hedging, apology, and disclaimers erode trust. If we can't answer, we say so clearly and tell them who can.

Voice principles

01

Verdict first

Every result leads with one of: Legal · Rx-only · Restricted · Banned. No preamble.

02

Bad news is a feature

"Banned" is stated plainly, never softened. Immediately followed by what to do instead.

03

Show the work

Every claim names the source agency and verification date, inline, not in a footer.

04

Speed is trust

UI copy acknowledges time: "Answered in 1.8s", "Verified Apr 11", "Act by May 06".

03 · Color

Brand lane and status lane live in strict separation.

Orange, blue, and gold are brand identity — they appear in navigation, CTAs, trust elements, and highlights. The four status colors (sage, sky, amber, rose) appear only in compliance results. Never decorative. Never cross-contaminated.

04 · Status system

Four lanes. Never decorative.

Every compliance verdict lands in exactly one of four visual lanes. Each uses triple encoding — color + icon + text — so the status is legible for a user with low vision, on a grayscale screen, or at a glance across a 24-item list. If you reach for one of these colors and it's not a compliance result, use a brand color instead.

Legal
Permitted in personal quantities. No prescription or permit required.
bg: emerald-50 #ECF3EC fg: emerald-700 #2F6B3F
E.g. Ibuprofen 200mg in Germany
Prescription-Only
Legal with a valid prescription. Carry in original pharmacy packaging.
bg: sky-50 #E4EEF6 fg: sky-700 #2D6A92
E.g. Sertraline 50mg in Japan
Restricted
Permit, certificate, or quantity cap required. Has a deadline.
bg: amber-50 #F7ECD4 fg: amber-700 #8A5E14
E.g. Codeine 30mg in UAE
Banned
Prohibited. No permit path. Must switch or leave behind.
bg: rose-50 #F5DFD8 fg: rose-700 #9C3D2A
E.g. Adderall in Japan
Terracotta, not red. "Banned" uses a muted rose/terracotta — firm but not panic-inducing. Pure red triggers fight-or-flight in users who are already anxious; it reduces comprehension of the next-steps panel immediately below the verdict.

Do / Don't

✓ Do · Use the status lane only for compliance verdicts
Banned Restricted Legal
Status colors carry one meaning per lane. Triple-encoded: color + dot + text label.
✗ Don't · Use status colors for decoration or secondary messaging
NEW
A rose "Cancel" button or amber "NEW" pill dilutes the status system. Use ink/ghost buttons and brand-colored pills instead.
05 · Typography

Montserrat structures. Open Sans substantiates.

Two-family pairing with a strict rule: Montserrat handles anything scanned — headlines, nav, buttons, badges, quantity data. Open Sans handles anything read — body copy, compliance details, source citations, permit instructions. JetBrains Mono is reserved for sources, IDs, and timestamps.

07 · Iconography

2px stroke. Rounded caps. Square bounds.

CheckOrbit uses a consistent 2px-stroke, 24×24 icon grid with rounded line caps and joins. Icons carry the semantics of their neighboring status lane — never invent new shapes for status; use the same four verdict icons.

08 · Components · Buttons

Four roles. Pill geometry.

All buttons are 999px-radius pills. Role is signaled by fill color, not shape. Primary for the one main action on a screen; Ghost for secondary; Gold for premium upsell; Blue for trust-adjacent "resolve this" flows.

12 · Components · Verdicts

The headline of every result.

The verdict block is the single most important element on the result page. It lives directly under the route/title pair, uses one of four status-lane backgrounds, leads with a Montserrat 800 label, and ends with one sentence that names the legal basis.

Banned — do not carry.
Amphetamine-based stimulants are prohibited under Japan's Stimulants Control Act. There is no tourist permit available.
Restricted — permit required.
Codeine supply over 10 days requires a Yakkan Shoumei certificate. Lead time: 14 business days.
Prescription-only.
Carry sertraline in original pharmacy-labeled bottle. No certificate required.
Legal. Pack as you normally would.
Ibuprofen is OTC in Germany. Personal supply up to 2 months permitted without declaration.

14 · Motion & loading

Fast confidence, narrated.

CheckOrbit's loading states are not generic spinners. They narrate the verification in mono, reinforcing "we are checking real sources right now". Target time-to-result: under 2 seconds on the search path.

Motion rules

∿

Ease-out everywhere

Transitions 150–200ms, cubic-bezier(0.2, 0.9, 0.3, 1). Never ease-in.

↓

Press depth only

Buttons translateY(1px) on :active. No scale, no glow.

✦

Narrated loaders

Any wait over 600ms gets a verification-step sub-caption cycling through source IDs.

◐

Respect reduced-motion

prefers-reduced-motion kills spin, fade, translate. Loader becomes static with ticking text only.

15 · Accessibility

WCAG 2.1 AA, with one extra rule.

Because CheckOrbit answers medical questions to anxious travelers at 2 AM, we add a rule above AA: every status must survive monochrome. Color alone is never the carrier of a verdict — there is always an icon and a text label.

3×

Triple encoding

Status = color + icon + text label. A rose-blind user reading in grayscale still sees "✗ Banned".

44

Touch targets ≥ 44px

Every tappable control, including nav buttons and medication cards, hits the 44×44 minimum.

AA

Contrast

Body text meets 4.5:1 on cream. Badges meet 4.5:1 between label and status background. Source mono on dark meets 7:1.

⌨

Keyboard-first

Search flow is fully keyboardable: Tab through fields, arrows in typeahead, Enter to submit.

⌘

Focus rings kept

Never `outline: 0` without replacement. Focus uses a 2px orange ring offset 2px.

◑

Reduced motion

prefers-reduced-motion disables all animation and transitions immediately.
