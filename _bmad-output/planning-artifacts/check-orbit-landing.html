FAQ

Questions before you pack.

Straight answers on how Check Orbit works, what it costs, and what it won't do.

Is Check Orbit medical advice?

No. Check Orbit summarizes publicly available import regulations and helps you organize your existing prescriptions. It doesn't diagnose, prescribe, or replace your doctor. For dosing changes, always talk to your prescriber.

How does it know the laws in every country?

Our regulatory team updates a database of controlled-substance schedules, import quantities, and declaration rules from official sources — including INCB, national health ministries, and embassy guidance. Every country is re-verified at least quarterly.

Will it work without internet at the border?

Yes. Once you check a trip, your medication list, customs forms, and doctor's letter are saved offline. You can show them from your phone even in airplane mode.

What about privacy? My meds are sensitive.

Your medication list is encrypted on your device and never sold, shared, or used for advertising. You can delete your account and all associated data from Settings in one tap.

How much does it cost?

The first trip check is free. After that, Check Orbit is $4.99/month or $39/year — unlimited trips, unlimited medications. Students and travelers on financial aid get it free; email support to apply.

Does it handle refills and pharmacies abroad?

For many destinations, yes. Check Orbit shows verified pharmacies near your trip and flags which of your medications are available by generic name locally. We don't ship medications ourselves.

What if a medication is illegal at my destination?

We flag it before you fly and — when possible — suggest legal alternatives to discuss with your doctor. If there's no alternative, we'll walk you through applying for a medical exemption permit, which some countries offer.
