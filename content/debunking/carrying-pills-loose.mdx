## The Myth

Weekly pillboxes are a staple of domestic travel. Many travelers assume the same convenience applies internationally: sort your medications by day, toss them in your carry-on, and go.

**Across dozens of countries, this is treated as evidence of drug trafficking.**

## Why Pillboxes Are a Problem

When customs officers encounter loose, unlabeled pills, they cannot verify:

- What the substance is
- Whether it's legal in their country
- Whether you have a valid prescription
- Whether the quantity is for personal use

In countries with strict drug enforcement — Japan, Singapore, UAE, Indonesia, Thailand — unidentified pills trigger mandatory inspection. You may be held while the pills are lab-tested, a process that can take days.

## Countries Where This Is Especially Risky

- **Japan** — Amphetamine-class medications (including Adderall) are banned outright. Loose pills matching stimulant profiles can lead to arrest.
- **UAE** — CBD and codeine are controlled. A single loose pill containing a banned substance can result in a 4-year prison sentence.
- **Singapore** — Carries the death penalty for trafficking certain quantities of controlled substances. Loose pills without documentation are treated with extreme suspicion.

## What You Should Do Instead

1. **Keep every medication in its original pharmacy container** — the label is your proof
2. **If you must use a pillbox**, also carry the original labeled bottles in the same bag
3. **Bring a doctor's letter** listing each medication, dosage, and medical necessity
4. **Research before you fly** — what's over-the-counter at home may be illegal at your destination

## Check Before You Pack

Verify every medication in your travel kit against your destination's regulations.
